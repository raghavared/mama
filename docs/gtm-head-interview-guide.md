# GTM Head Interview Guide & Evaluation Rubric

## Interview Structure Overview
Total Time: ~5 hours across multiple sessions
- Initial Screen: 30 minutes
- Take-Home Case Study: 2-3 hours
- Technical Deep Dive: 60 minutes
- Cross-Functional Panel: 3x45 minutes
- Executive Interview: 45 minutes

---

## Round 1: Initial Screen (30 minutes)
**Interviewer**: Hiring Manager or Head of People

### Questions

#### Opening (5 min)
1. Walk me through your career journey and what brings you to MAMA.
2. What excites you most about the intersection of AI and marketing?

#### GTM Experience (10 min)
3. Tell me about a product you took from 0 to 1 from a GTM perspective.
   - *Listen for: Strategy, metrics, challenges overcome*
4. What's your approach to defining an ICP in a new market?
   - *Listen for: Data-driven approach, iteration, validation methods*

#### AI/Content Marketing (10 min)
5. How do you see AI changing content marketing in the next 2 years?
   - *Listen for: Vision, understanding of current landscape*
6. What's the biggest challenge in attributing content to revenue?
   - *Listen for: Technical understanding, experience with attribution*

#### Culture Fit (5 min)
7. Describe your ideal work environment and team dynamics.
8. Questions from candidate

### Evaluation Criteria
- [ ] **Communication**: Clear, concise, structured thinking
- [ ] **Relevant Experience**: B2B SaaS, AI/ML products, content marketing
- [ ] **Strategic Thinking**: Big picture vision with execution mindset
- [ ] **Culture Fit**: Startup mentality, bias for action
- [ ] **Interest Level**: Genuine excitement about MAMA's mission

**Pass Criteria**: 3+ of 5 criteria met strongly

## Round 2: Take-Home Case Study

### Assignment
**Time Limit**: 3 hours maximum
**Format**: Slide deck (10-15 slides) + 1-page executive summary

### Prompt
"You've just been hired as GTM Head at MAMA. After reviewing our documentation and trying the product demo, create a 90-day GTM strategy that addresses:

1. **Market Analysis** (2-3 slides)
   - Define our ICP with specific company/persona characteristics
   - Identify top 3 competitors and our differentiation
   - Size the TAM/SAM/SOM

2. **Positioning & Messaging** (2-3 slides)
   - Craft our value proposition
   - Define 3 key messages for different buyer personas
   - Create a positioning statement

3. **Channel Strategy** (2-3 slides)
   - Recommend top 3 acquisition channels with rationale
   - Propose content strategy for each channel
   - Define success metrics per channel

4. **Quick Wins** (2-3 slides)
   - List 3 experiments you'd run in first 30 days
   - Expected outcomes and success criteria
   - Resource requirements

5. **Measurement Framework** (2-3 slides)
   - Define North Star metric
   - Build attribution model outline
   - List key leading/lagging indicators

Bonus: Include one 'unconventional' growth tactic specific to AI-powered content generation."

### Evaluation Rubric

#### Market Understanding (25 points)
- **Exceptional (22-25)**: Deep research, specific ICP, quantified TAM with sources
- **Strong (18-21)**: Good ICP definition, competitor analysis, reasonable TAM
- **Adequate (14-17)**: Basic market sizing, generic personas
- **Below Bar (0-13)**: Vague or unrealistic market assessment

#### Strategic Thinking (25 points)
- **Exceptional (22-25)**: Clear strategy with unique insights, connects AI capabilities to market needs
- **Strong (18-21)**: Solid strategy, good positioning, logical flow
- **Adequate (14-17)**: Standard GTM approach, limited differentiation
- **Below Bar (0-13)**: Unclear strategy, generic recommendations

#### Execution Planning (25 points)
- **Exceptional (22-25)**: Specific, measurable experiments with clear hypothesis
- **Strong (18-21)**: Good tactical plans, realistic timelines
- **Adequate (14-17)**: High-level tactics, some specificity
- **Below Bar (0-13)**: Vague plans, no clear execution path

#### Data & Measurement (25 points)
- **Exceptional (22-25)**: Sophisticated attribution model, clear KPIs tied to business outcomes
- **Strong (18-21)**: Good metrics framework, understands attribution
- **Adequate (14-17)**: Basic metrics, some measurement gaps
- **Below Bar (0-13)**: Weak measurement approach, vanity metrics

**Pass Criteria**: 70+ total points, no category below 14 points

## Round 3: Technical Deep Dive (60 minutes)
**Interviewer**: Current GTM/Marketing Leader + Data Lead

### Part 1: Case Study Discussion (30 min)

1. Walk us through your thinking on the ICP definition.
   - *Drill into: Research methods, validation approach*

2. How did you arrive at your channel recommendations?
   - *Drill into: CAC/LTV thinking, scalability*

3. Explain your attribution model in detail.
   - *Drill into: Multi-touch attribution, technical implementation*

4. What assumptions are you making that could be wrong?
   - *Listen for: Self-awareness, risk mitigation*

### Part 2: Technical Scenarios (20 min)

5. **Scenario**: Our content is getting high engagement but low conversions. Walk me through your diagnostic process.
   - *Listen for: Systematic thinking, hypothesis generation, testing approach*

6. **Data Question**: We have 10,000 pieces of content with performance data. How would you identify which content types drive pipeline?
   - *Listen for: SQL/analytics knowledge, statistical thinking, cohort analysis*

7. **Integration Question**: How would you connect our content generation to Salesforce for attribution?
   - *Listen for: Technical understanding, API knowledge, data flow design*

### Part 3: Behavioral Questions (10 min)

8. Tell me about a time you had to pivot a GTM strategy mid-execution.
   - *STAR method: Situation, Task, Action, Result*

9. Describe a situation where marketing and sales weren't aligned. How did you fix it?
   - *Listen for: Cross-functional leadership, communication skills*

### Evaluation Criteria
- [ ] **Technical Depth**: Understanding of martech, analytics, attribution
- [ ] **Problem-Solving**: Structured approach, hypothesis-driven
- [ ] **Data Fluency**: Comfort with SQL, analytics tools, statistical concepts
- [ ] **Communication**: Explains technical concepts clearly
- [ ] **Learning Agility**: Incorporates feedback, asks clarifying questions

## Round 4: Cross-Functional Panel (3x45 minutes)

### Panel A: Engineering Leader (45 min)

#### Technical Collaboration
1. How do you typically work with engineering teams?
2. Describe your experience with APIs and data pipelines.
3. How would you prioritize marketing features on the product roadmap?

#### MAMA-Specific
4. Which AI agent would you enhance first for GTM purposes and why?
5. How would you structure data requirements for the analytics you need?
6. What's your experience with real-time data systems?

### Panel B: Product Leader (45 min)

#### Product Marketing
1. How do you balance product roadmap input with GTM needs?
2. Tell me about launching a technical product to non-technical buyers.
3. How do you gather and incorporate customer feedback into positioning?

#### MAMA-Specific
4. How would you position MAMA against traditional marketing automation tools?
5. What product features would unlock the most GTM value?
6. How would you approach pricing and packaging strategy?

### Panel C: Sales Leader (45 min)

#### Sales Enablement
1. How do you enable sales teams to sell complex/technical products?
2. What's your approach to building sales collateral?
3. How do you measure marketing's impact on sales velocity?

#### MAMA-Specific
4. What objections do you anticipate customers having about AI-generated content?
5. How would you structure a pilot program for enterprise customers?
6. What sales plays would you develop for different segments?

### Evaluation Matrix

| Competency | Engineering Panel | Product Panel | Sales Panel |
|------------|------------------|---------------|-------------|
| Technical Aptitude | Primary focus | Secondary | Tertiary |
| Strategic Thinking | Secondary | Primary | Secondary |
| Collaboration | Primary | Primary | Primary |
| Communication | Secondary | Secondary | Primary |
| Domain Expertise | Tertiary | Secondary | Primary |

**Pass Criteria**: Average score of 3.5+ across all panels (1-5 scale)

## Round 5: Executive Interview (45 minutes)
**Interviewer**: CEO/Founder

### Vision & Strategy (20 min)
1. Where do you see the future of AI-powered marketing heading?
2. How would you build MAMA into a category-defining company?
3. What's your perspective on PLG vs. enterprise sales for our product?
4. How would you think about international expansion?

### Leadership & Culture (15 min)
5. How do you build high-performing teams?
6. Tell me about a time you failed and what you learned.
7. How do you balance perfectionism with shipping quickly?
8. What's your management philosophy?

### Business Acumen (10 min)
9. Walk me through unit economics for a typical B2B SaaS company.
10. How would you structure our pricing model?
11. What metrics would you present to our board?

### Final Assessment
- [ ] **Vision Alignment**: Shares our ambition and values
- [ ] **Leadership Potential**: Can grow with the company
- [ ] **Business Judgment**: Makes smart tradeoffs
- [ ] **Culture Add**: Brings unique perspective while fitting culture
- [ ] **Hunger**: Genuinely excited about the opportunity

## Reference Check Questions

### Performance & Results
1. How would you rank [Candidate] among GTM leaders you've worked with?
2. Can you give specific examples of revenue impact they drove?
3. What was their biggest win? Biggest miss?

### Work Style
4. How do they handle ambiguity and changing priorities?
5. Describe their relationship with sales and product teams.
6. How do they respond to feedback and failure?

### Growth Potential
7. What's their superpower? What's their kryptonite?
8. Would you hire them again? Why/why not?
9. What environment would they thrive in? Struggle in?

### Red Flags to Listen For
- Lack of specific metrics/results
- Poor cross-functional collaboration
- Inability to handle pressure
- Not data-driven
- Slow execution speed

## Scoring Framework

### Overall Scoring Matrix

| Round | Weight | Pass Threshold |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| Initial Screen | 10% | 3/5 criteria |
| Case Study | 25% | 70/100 points |
| Technical Deep Dive | 25% | 4/5 criteria |
| Cross-Functional Panel | 25% | 3.5/5 average |
| Executive Interview | 15% | 4/5 criteria |

### Final Decision Framework

**Strong Hire**:
- Total weighted score: 85%+
- No red flags in references
- Exceptional in 2+ areas
- Culture add confirmed

**Hire**:
- Total weighted score: 70-84%
- Minor concerns addressable with support
- Strong in 1+ areas
- Good culture fit

**No Hire**:
- Total weighted score: <70%
- Major concerns in references
- Below bar in any critical area
- Culture misalignment

### Calibration Questions for Debrief
1. Can this person take us from $0 to $10M ARR?
2. Will they raise the bar for future GTM hires?
3. Can they grow into a CMO/CRO role?
4. Would the team be excited to work with them?
5. Do they have the hustle for early-stage?
